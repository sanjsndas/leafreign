Gardening Tips for Everyone

1. Know Your Soil

Understanding your soil type is crucial for successful gardening. Test your soil to determine its pH and nutrient levels.

2. Choose Native Plants

Native plants are adapted to your local climate and soil conditions, making them easier to care for and more beneficial to local wildlife.

3. Water Wisely

Water your plants early in the morning or late in the evening to minimize evaporation. Consider using drip irrigation for efficiency.

4. Mulch for Moisture

Applying mulch helps retain soil moisture, suppress weeds, and improve soil quality as it breaks down.

5. Rotate Your Crops

Crop rotation helps prevent soil depletion and reduces the risk of pests and diseases.

6. Prune Regularly

Regular pruning encourages healthy growth and can help prevent disease by improving air circulation.
