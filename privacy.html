Privacy Policy

At Leafreign, we are committed to protecting your privacy and personal information

Our Privacy Commitment

Leafreign ("we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy will inform you as to how we look after your personal data when you visit our website (regardless of where you visit it from) and tell you about your privacy rights and how the law protects you.

This policy was last updated on June 10, 2023 and may change from time to time. Please check this page regularly to stay informed about our privacy practices.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Identification Information

We may collect personal identification information such as name, email address, phone number, mailing address, and other contact details when users fill out forms on our site, subscribe to newsletters, or request services. This information is used solely for the purpose of providing requested services and communicating with clients about their gardening projects.

1.2 Non-Personal Identification Information

Whenever users interact with our website, we may collect non-personal identification information including browser details, operating system, internet service providers, and similar technical information. This data helps us improve user experience and site functionality without identifying individual visitors.

1.3 Website Usage Data

Our website automatically collects information about how visitors access and use our site ("usage data"). This usage data may include page views, navigation paths, time spent on pages, and other analytical information that helps us understand how to improve our services and website functionality.

2. How We Use Collected Information

2.1 Providing Services

The primary use of collected personal information is to provide requested gardening services, respond to inquiries, and facilitate communication between clients and our team. This includes scheduling consultations, providing estimates, and delivering contracted landscaping services.

2.2 Personalizing User Experience

We may use aggregated information to understand how our users as a group use our site's resources, allowing us to improve content presentation, customize user experiences, and optimize our service offerings based on consumer preferences and browsing patterns.

2.3 Improving Our Website

Visitor feedback helps us continuously improve our website offerings. We analyze user behavior and preferences to identify technical issues, optimize performance, and enhance the overall quality of our digital presence to better serve our gardening service customers.

2.4 Sending Periodic Emails

For users who opt-in to our mailing list, email addresses may be used to send company news, gardening tips, service updates, or promotional materials. Each email includes an unsubscribe option, and we comply with all anti-spam regulations.

2.5 Processing Payments

When applicable, collected financial information is used solely to process payments for services. All payment processing occurs through secure third-party providers, and Leafreign does not store complete credit card information on our servers.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

3.1 Contractual Necessity

Much of our data processing is necessary to fulfill our contractual obligations to clients. When you request gardening services, we need certain personal information to communicate with you, provide quotations, schedule service dates, and complete the agreed work.

3.2 Legitimate Interests

We process some data based on our legitimate business interests, such as marketing similar services to existing clients, preventing fraud, ensuring network security, improving our services, and maintaining business operations, as long as these interests don't override individual privacy rights.

3.3 Legal Compliance

In certain circumstances, we may need to process personal data to comply with legal or regulatory obligations, such as tax reporting requirements, responding to legal process, or as otherwise required by applicable laws.

3.4 Consent

Where required by law, or in situations where other legal bases don't apply, we will ask for consent before processing personal data. For example, this applies to certain cookies and marketing communications where consent is the lawful basis under applicable privacy laws.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 Service Providers

We may engage trusted third-party companies to facilitate our services ("Service Providers"), such as hosting providers, email service providers, payment processors, and subcontracted gardening professionals. These third parties have access only to information needed to perform their functions.

4.2 Business Transfers

If Leafreign is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your personal data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any transfer and provide options regarding your data as required by applicable laws.

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your personal data when required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., court orders, subpoenas). Such disclosures may be necessary for compliance with legal obligations or protection of our rights.

4.4 Aggregate Information

We may share aggregated demographic information not linked to personal identification information with business partners, advertisers, and researchers. This helps us understand market trends and improve overall gardening service offerings without compromising individual privacy.

5. International Data Transfers

5.1 Transfer Mechanisms

Some of our service providers may be located outside your jurisdiction. Where we transfer data to countries without adequate protection laws, we implement appropriate safeguards as required by regulations, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or Privacy Shield certification.

5.2 Data Storage Locations

Leafreign primarily stores data within the United States. However, our cloud service providers may store backups in multiple locations worldwide to ensure data redundancy and business continuity, always maintaining appropriate security measures regardless of location.

6. Data Security Measures

6.1 Security Protocols

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. This includes encryption, access controls, secure authentication methods, and regular security audits.

6.2 Employee Training

Our employees receive regular training on security and privacy best practices. Access to personal data is limited to personnel who need it to perform their jobs and is protected through role-based permissions and secure authentication protocols.

6.3 Incident Response

We maintain an incident response plan to address potential data breaches. In the event of a security incident, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by law and take appropriate steps to mitigate harm.

7. Data Retention Practices

7.1 Retention Periods

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including satisfying legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Typical retention periods range from 3-7 years depending on data type and legal obligations.

7.2 Anonymization

When data is no longer needed for its original purpose but can be valuable for statistical analysis or business improvement, we may anonymize it so that individuals cannot be identified from the remaining information.

8. User Rights and Choices

8.1 Access and Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal data we hold about you and to request corrections if you believe that any information is inaccurate or incomplete. We will respond to such requests within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable laws.

8.2 Data Portability

You have the right to request a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. This allows you to transfer your data to another service provider if you choose to do so.

8.3 Right to Erasure

You have the right to request the deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or if you withdraw your consent on which the processing is based.

8.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data under certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of the data or when you have objected to processing.

8.5 Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or if the processing is based on legitimate interests. We will cease processing your data unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

9.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to the site owners.

9.2 Types of Cookies We Use

We use session cookies to manage user sessions and persistent cookies to remember user preferences. Additionally, we may use third-party cookies for analytics and advertising purposes.

9.3 Managing Cookies

You can choose to accept or decline cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies if you prefer. However, this may prevent you from taking full advantage of the website.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

10.1 Updates

We may update our privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting a notice on our website.

10.2 Review Period

We encourage you to review this privacy policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information. Your continued use of our services after any modifications to the policy will constitute your acknowledgment of the modifications and your consent to abide by and be bound by the modified policy.
